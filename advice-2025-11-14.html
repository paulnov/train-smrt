Analysis

  • A 15-12-8 sequence taken to failure with only 60 s between sets usually starts around 65–70% 1RM for the first set, but cumulative fatigue drives the last set closer to 80–85% relative intensity because you’re effectively at 0–1 reps in reserve (RPE 9.5–10). That’s materially heavier—in terms of metabolic and neural stress—than the RPE ≈7 target for tonight’s maintenance work.
  • To hit the prescribed 70–80% 1RM / RPE 7 zone, you want ~3 reps in reserve (RIR 3) when the set ends. Practically, use a load you could take to 12 reps fresh but stop at 8–9, or a load you could take to 10 but stop at 7. This trims fatigue while keeping enough mechanical tension to maintain muscle cross-section and tendon stiffness.
  • Diaphragmatic breathing = low, 360° rib-cage expansion using the diaphragm and lower intercostals rather than shallow chest breaths. Between supersets, 3–4 slow cycles (≈4 s inhale, 2 s hold, 6 s exhale) drops sympathetic drive, restores intra-abdominal pressure, and improves bar velocity on the next set. Treat it as part of rest hygiene, not a separate session.

Proposed revision

  1. Load translation: Take whatever load makes your third set of 8 reps a near-failure grind and reduce it by roughly 10–15%. Example: if 55 lb dumbbells give you 15-12-8 to failure, run ~45–50 lb for tonight’s 2×8–10 prescription. Same logic for goblet squats, RDLs, rows, presses, and step-ups.
  2. Gauge RPE via RIR: Mid-set ask, “How many perfect-form reps remain?”; stop with 2–3 in the tank. If you accidentally hit RIR 1, cut the second set reps by two. Use this for each move so the entire circuit stays sub-threshold.
  3. Breathing protocol between supersets: Stand tall or lie supine, one hand on lower ribs. Inhale through the nose for 4 s feeling the belly-lateral ribs expand, hold 2 s to set intra-abdominal pressure, exhale through pursed lips for 6 s letting ribs descend. Two to four cycles (20–30 s) is enough to re-oxygenate, drop HR by ~10 bpm, and cue trunk stability before the next set.
  4. Logging: Note the load, reps, and perceived RIR. This creates a personal lookup table so that “2×8 @ RIR 3” immediately maps to percentages without formal 1RM testing.
